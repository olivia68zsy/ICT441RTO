PSP60916 - Advanced Diploma of Interpreting (LOTE-English)
See more about PSP60916
This qualification covers the competencies required for independent and self-directed work as an interpreting practitioner. It reflects the skills and knowledge required to interpret from a source language to a target language in complex dialogue and monologue settings where the physical elements of the setting or number of parties involved can challenge the capacity of the interpreter to manage the discourse. The interpreter will require advanced skills in retention and recall and may be required to switch modes.

See more about TAE80113
Adult language, literacy and numeracy skills development is provided by a range of training organisations in vocational education settings, workplaces and the community. Titles for job roles with responsibility for adult language, literacy and numeracy delivery and assessment vary across different organisational contexts. Possible job titles include: practitioner, Workplace English Language and Literacy (WELL) program; teacher, Language, Literacy and Numeracy Program (LLNP); concurrent assistance teacher, training organization; adult literacy and numeracy teacher, training organization; trainer and assessor, training organization; program coordinator, adult community education organization.

CUA60520 - Advanced Diploma of Music
See more about CUA60520
This qualification reflects the role of individuals who use wide-ranging analytical, technical, creative, conceptual and managerial skills in their chosen field in the music industry. Their knowledge base may be specialised or broad, and they may operate in their industry sector with autonomy, judgement, and defined responsibility. These individuals are sometimes accountable for group outcomes. They may work in music performance, music creation and composition, music tuition, sound production, or music business contexts.

Simple Lesson Plan

Course Overview

This course aims to cultivate students' mastery of the core theories and skills of modern information technology, and apply them to practice. Through demonstration teaching methods, this course will provide ample opportunities for students to use information technology knowledge in real-world environments. By collaborating with industry partners to carry out projects, students will learn how to design, develop, test, and publish different types of computer applications.

Training objectives

  • Understand the core theories of modern information technology.
  • Master common tools and skills in information technology.
  • Learn to design, develop, test, and publish different types of computer applications.
  • Develop skills for teamwork, communication, and problem-solving.

Course Arrangement

First Week

  • Introduce course overview, educational objectives, and performance evaluation.
  • Discuss the trends and challenges of modern information technology.
  • Overview and practice of demonstration teaching methods.

Week 2

  • Introduce computer composition and operating system principles.
  • Use Linux for basic operations and scripting.
  • Example project 1: Application development in a Linux environment.

Week 3

  • Introduce database design and management.
  • Use MySQL for data modeling and query optimization.
  • Example project 2: MySQL based web application development.

Fourth Week

  • Introduce web development technologies and frameworks.
  • Use Django for web application development.
  • Example project 3: Developing an e-commerce website using Django.

Week 5

  • Introduce mobile application development technology and platform.
  • Use React Native for mobile application development.
  • Example project 4: Cross platform mobile application development based on React Native.

Week 6

  • Introduce artificial intelligence and machine learning technologies.
  • Train and predict machine learning models using Python.
  • Example Project 5: Data Mining and Analysis Based on Machine Learning.

Week 7

  • Summarize the course content and learning outcomes.
  • Evaluate the practical results of partner projects.
  • Course feedback and evaluation.

Performance evaluation

  • Projects and assignments: 40%
  • Final exam: 30%
  • Partner projects: 20%
  • Participation and Discussion: 10%

The language requirements for the above courses are IELTS 6.5 (6.0) or TOEFL 79 (LR16, W21, S18). Teachers will provide demonstrations as needed during the course to support students' learning outcomes.
